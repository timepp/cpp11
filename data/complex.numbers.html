[complex.numbers]26.4 Complex numbers

1The header <complex> defines a class template, and numerous functions for representing and manipulating complex numbers.

2The effect of instantiating the template complex for any type other than float, double, or long double is unspecified. The specializations complex<float>, complex<double>, and complex<long double> are literal types (3.9).

3If the result of a function is not mathematically defined or not in the range of representable values for its type, the behavior is undefined.

4If z is an lvalue expression of type cv std::complex<T> then:

  • the expression reinterpret_cast<cv T(&)[2]>(z) shall be well-formed,
  • reinterpret_cast<cv T(&)[2]>(z)[0] shall designate the real part of z, and
  • reinterpret_cast<cv T(&)[2]>(z)[1] shall designate the imaginary part of z.

Moreover, if a is an expression of type cv std::complex<T>* and the expression a[i] is well-defined for an integer expression i, then:

  • reinterpret_cast<cv T*>(a)[2*i] shall designate the real part of a[i], and
  • reinterpret_cast<cv T*>(a)[2*i + 1] shall designate the imaginary part of a[i].
