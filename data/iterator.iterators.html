[iterator.iterators]24.2.2 Iterator

1The Iterator requirements form the basis of the iterator concept taxonomy; every iterator satisfies the Iterator requirements. This set of requirements specifies operations for dereferencing and incrementing an iterator. Most algorithms will require additional operations to read (24.2.3) or write (24.2.4) values, or to provide a richer set of iterator movements (24.2.5, 24.2.6, 24.2.7).

2A type X satisfies the Iterator requirements if:

  • X satisfies the CopyConstructible, CopyAssignable, and Destructible requirements (17.6.3.1) and lvalues of type X are swappable (17.6.3.2), and
  • the expressions in Table 106 are valid and have the indicated semantics.
