[unexpected]D.11.4 unexpected 

[[noreturn]] void unexpected();

1Remarks: Called by the implementation when a function exits via an exception not allowed by its exception-specification (15.5.2), in effect after evaluating the throw-expression (D.11.1). May also be called directly by the program.

2Effects: Calls the current unexpected_handler function. [ Note: A default unexpected_handler is always considered a callable handler in this context. — end note ]
